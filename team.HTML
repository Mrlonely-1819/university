EVENTS

Events are one of the most important classes of entities in our everyday psychology. They are the “things” of experience just as much as objects, sounds, and people. As we go about our lives, our minds and brains process information from an imposing number of sources. Major events have the potential to have positive effects on the people and communities that interact with them. They have the power to mobilise large numbers of people and create meaningful impacts on their lives in a number of different ways.
